Geometry & Coordinate Planes

From classic Euclidean dimensions to graphing axes.

1. Triangles & Pythagorean Theorem

The interior angles of any planar triangle always sum up to exactly 180°.

For Right-Angled Triangles, the square of the longest side (hypotenuse c) is the sum of the squares of the other two sides (a and b).

a² + b² = c²

Common Pythagorean triples: 3-4-5, 5-12-13, 8-15-17. Memorizing these saves incredible time in JAMB!

2. Circle Theorems

  • Angle at the centre: The angle subtended by an arc at the centre is double the angle subtended by it at any remaining part of the circle's circumference.
  • Angles in the same segment: Angles in the same circle segment are equal.
  • Angle in a semicircle: The angle within a semicircle forms exactly 90°.
  • Cyclic Quadrilaterals: Opposite angles in a cyclic quad sum to 180°.
  • Alternate Segment Theorem: The angle between a tangent and a chord through the point of contact equals the angle in the alternate segment.

3. Coordinate Geometry

Operating on the Cartesian plane (x, y) involves essential distance and midpoint calculations.
