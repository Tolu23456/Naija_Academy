Data collection matrices, variance calculation, and independent events.

1. Measures of Central Tendency

The statistical attempts to find the "center" or typical value of a dataset.

Measure Formula/Definition
Mean Σfx / Σf (Sum of all values divided by count)
Median The middle value when sorted. If even array, average of the two middle elements.
Mode Value with the highest frequency. Bimodal implies two modes.

2. Measures of Dispersion

How spread out the data is relative to the mean.

  • Range: Highest value minus Lowest value.
  • Variance (σ²): The average of the squared differences from the Mean.
  • Standard Deviation (σ): The square root of the variance. This guarantees the dispersion is in the same unit as the original data array!

3. Probability Theory

Probability ranges strictly from 0 (impossible) to 1 (certain).

Mutually Exclusive Events:

Cannot happen at the same time (e.g., rolling a 1 and rolling a 6 on one die). Use the Addition Law: P(A OR B) = P(A) + P(B)

Independent Events:

Outcome of one doesn't affect the other (e.g., flipping a coin, then rolling a die). Use the Multiplication Law: P(A AND B) = P(A) × P(B)
