1. Linear Equations

Linear equations form the foundation of Algebra. They are mathematical statements asserting the equality of two expressions that have a maximum degree of 1.

Example: Solve for x in 3x - 5 = 16

Step 1: 3x = 16 + 5
Step 2: 3x = 21
Step 3: x = 21 / 3
x = 7

2. Quadratic Equations

A quadratic equation is a second-order polynomial equation in a single variable x, with a non-zero leading coefficient.

ax² + bx + c = 0

Solving by Factoring

Find two numbers that multiply to c and add up to b.

Example: x² + 5x + 6 = 0 factors into (x+2)(x+3) = 0, meaning x = -2 or x = -3.

The Quadratic Formula

x = [-b ± √(b² - 4ac)] / 2a

3. Inequalities

Inequalities show the relationship between two non-equal expressions. The crucial rule is: When you multiply or divide an inequality by a negative number, you must flip the inequality sign!

-2x > 10
x < 10 / -2
x < -5
