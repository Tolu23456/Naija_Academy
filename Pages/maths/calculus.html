Calculus Intensity

The mathematics of continuous change.

1. Differentiation (Finding the Gradient)

Differentiation identifies the exact rate of change of a curve at any given point.

The Power Rule

If y = anⁿ, then the derivative dy/dx = n × a × x♠ (where power becomes n-1).

Example: If y = 3x&sup4;, dy/dx = 4(3)x³ = 12x³

  • Stationary Points: Found where dy/dx = 0.
  • Maximum/Minimum: Determined by the second derivative d²y/dx² (Negative = Max, Positive = Min).

2. Integration (Finding the Area)

Integration is the exact reverse of differentiation. It is used to find the area under a curve.

∫ axⁿ dx = [a × xⁿ+¹] / (n+1) + C

Never forget the constant of integration + C for indefinite integrals!
