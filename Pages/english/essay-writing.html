Essay & Letter Writing

Securing maximum points in the WAEC composition segment.

1. Formal vs Informal Letters

Formal (Official) Letters

  • TWO Addresses: Writer's (Top-right) and Receiver's (Bottom-left/Left margin).
  • Salutation: Dear Sir / Madam,
  • Title: Required (e.g. COMPLAINT ABOUT...)
  • Sign-off: Yours faithfully, [Signature], [Full Name]
  • No slang, contractions (can't/won't), or emotional greetings.

Informal (Friendly) Letters

  • ONE Address: Writer's (Top-right).
  • Salutation: Dear John, / My dear sister,
  • Title: NONE.
  • Sign-off: Yours sincerely, / Best wishes, [First Name Only]
  • Contractions, idiomatic expressions, and casual tone allowed.

2. Argumentative Essays

A debate on paper. You must persuade the reader to accept your stance on a controversial topic.

  • Introduction: Hooks the reader, states the thesis, and clearly defines your stance (For or Against).
  • Body Paragraphs: Each must start with a strong Topic Sentence, followed by supporting evidence and elaborations. Use transition words (Furthermore, Consequently, On the contrary).
  • Rebuttal: Acknowledge the opposing view briefly, then crush it with stronger logic.
  • Conclusion: Summarize main points and deliver a final, powerful concluding thought.
