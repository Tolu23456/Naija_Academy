Comprehension & Summary

Advanced deduction, tone analysis, and conciseness strategies.

1. Strategies for Reading Passages

  • Skimming: Read the first and last sentences of every paragraph to grasp the main idea rapidly.
  • Scanning: Hunting for specific data points (dates, names, statistics) after reading the questions first.
  • Contextual Deduction: Figuring out unknown vocabulary by examining how the sentence around it behaves.

2. Analyzing Tone & Attitude

Authors encode their feelings into texts. Recognizing attitude is purely subjective but highly testable!

Sarcastic / Irony

Meaning the exact opposite of what is overtly stated.

Objective

Purely factual, neutral reporting without bias.

Didactic

Intentionally instructive or preaching a moral lesson.

3. Summary Writing Mastery

WAEC strictly penalizes for "lifting" (direct copying) from the text. To secure full marks:

  • Ensure the tense of your summary matches the question's preamble.
  • Eliminate all examples, statistics, analogies, and repetitions.
  • Use concise, short sentences starting with clear subjects.
