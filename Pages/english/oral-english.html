Oral English Course

Phonetics rules, emphatic stress, and articulation.

1. Vowels (Monophthongs & Diphthongs)

English contains 20 vowel sounds (12 pure vowels and 8 diphthongs "glides"). JAMB tests your ability to match words with identical sounds.

/æ/ vs /α:/

Cat vs Farther

Man vs Heart

/ei/ vs /ai/

Pay vs Byte

Weigh vs Kite

2. Consonant Sounds & Clusters

Pay special attention to silent letters and challenging fricatives/affricates.

  • /θ/ vs /ð/: Thin (unvoiced) vs Then (voiced)
  • /∫/ vs /t∫/: Shoe (fricative) vs Chew (affricate)
  • Silent letters: Comb, Who, Know, Receipt (The P is silent!).

3. Emphatic Stress

JAMB uses capitalization to indicate stress on a word, testing your ability to deduce the opposing question.

Statement: John stole the BLUE car.

Question implying this stress: Did John steal the RED car?

(The stress corrects the false assumption in the question!)
