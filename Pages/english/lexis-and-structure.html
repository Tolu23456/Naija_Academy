Lexis & Grammatical Structure

Idioms, syntax, contextual vocabulary, and collocations.

1. Idioms & Phrasal Verbs

Idioms are expressions whose meanings cannot be deduced from the literal definitions of the individual words. WAEC and JAMB use them heavily to test deep English comprehension.

"To let the cat out of the bag" To reveal a secret carelessly or by mistake.
"A piece of cake" Something very easy to do.
To look up to someone To respect and admire someone (Phrasal Verb).
To give in To surrender or concede.

2. Collocations

Words that naturally go together. Non-native speakers often struggle because direct translations from mother-tongues fail.

  • ✓ Committing suicide (NOT making suicide)
  • ✓ Making an effort (NOT doing an effort)
  • ✓ Having a shower (NOT doing a shower)

3. Synonyms & Antonyms

Synonyms are words nearest in meaning, while Antonyms are opposites. Always read the surrounding passage context to define the target word!

Example context check: Although the student was brilliant, he was somewhat lethargic (lazy/sluggish).
