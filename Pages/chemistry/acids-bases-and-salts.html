Acids, Bases & Salts Course

Understanding pH and neutralization.

1. Arrhenius & Brønsted-Lowry Theory

Acids

Produce Hydrogen ions (H+) in water (Arrhenius). Brønsted-Lowry defines them as Proton Donors.

Bases

Produce Hydroxide ions (OH¯) in water. Brønsted-Lowry defines them as Proton Acceptors.

A soluble base is called an Alkali.

2. Strength vs Concentration

  • Strong Acids: Dissociate completely in water (e.g., HCl, H&sub2;SO&sub4;, HNO&sub3;).
  • Weak Acids: Dissociate only partially (e.g., Ethanoic acid CH&sub3;COOH, Citric acid).
  • Concentrated vs Dilute: Refers to the ratio of acid molecules to water molecules, NOT the degree of dissociation! You can have a concentrated weak acid.

3. Neutralization Reactions

When an Acid reacts with a Base, it forms Salt and Water.

HCl + NaOH → NaCl + H&sub2;O
