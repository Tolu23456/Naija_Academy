Electrochemistry Intensive

Electricity originating from chemical reactions.

1. Electrolysis Basics

The chemical decomposition of certain substances (electrolytes) produced by passing an electric current through them.

Anode (Positive Electrode)

Anions (Negative ions) are attracted here.
Oxidation occurs (Loss of electrons)!

Cathode (Negative Electrode)

Cations (Positive ions) are attracted here.
Reduction occurs (Gain of electrons)!

Memory tool: PANIC (Positive is Anode, Negative Is Cathode) & OIL RIG (Oxidation Is Loss, Reduction Is Gain).

2. Faraday's Laws of Electrolysis

  • First Law: The mass of a substance deposited/liberated is directly proportional to the quantity of electricity (Q) passed. M ∝ Q = I × t
  • Second Law: When the same quantity of electricity is passed through different electrolytes, the masses deposited are proportional to their chemical equivalent masses.
Q = I × t (Charge = Current × time in seconds)
