Periodic Table Course

The map of all known elements and their periodic trends.

1. Elements Organization

Groups (Vertical columns)

Indicates the number of valence electrons. Elements in the same group have similar chemical properties.

Periods (Horizontal rows)

Indicates the total number of electron shells an atom possesses.

2. Key Nomenclature Blocks

  • Group 1: Alkali Metals (Highly reactive, 1 valence e-)
  • Group 2: Alkaline Earth Metals (Slightly less reactive, 2 valence e-)
  • Group 17: Halogens (Highly reactive non-metals, 7 valence e-)
  • Group 18: Noble Gases (Inert, full valence shell)
  • d-Block: Transition Metals (Variable oxidation states, colored compounds)

3. Periodic Trends

Understanding these trends across Periods (left to right) and down Groups is heavily tested in exams.

Trend Property Across a Period (→) Down a Group (↓)
Atomic Radius Decreases (more nuclear pull) Increases (new shells)
Ionization Energy Increases (harder to pull e-) Decreases (shielding effect)
Electronegativity Increases Decreases
