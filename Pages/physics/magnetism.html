Magnetism & Electromagnetism

Fields, forces, and induction.

1. Magnetic Fields

A magnetic field is a region around a magnetic material or a moving electric charge within which the force of magnetism acts.

  • Field lines ALWAYS travel from the North pole to the South pole externally.
  • Field lines NEVER cross one another.
  • The closer the lines, the stronger the magnetic field flux density.

2. The Motor Effect (Fleming's Left-Hand Rule)

When a current-carrying conductor is placed in a magnetic field, it experiences a force.

First Finger: Magnetic Field (N to S)

Second Finger: Current (Positive to Negative)

Thumb: Thrust / Force (Direction of Motion)

Force formula: F = BIL sinθ

3. Electromagnetic Induction (Faraday & Lenz)

Faraday's Law: The induced EMF in a closed circuit is directly proportional to the rate of change of magnetic flux linkage.

Lenz's Law: The direction of the induced current is always such as to oppose the change producing it. (This is a consequence of conservation of energy!).
