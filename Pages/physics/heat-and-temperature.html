Thermal Physics

Heat, temperature, and macro-state behaviors of gases.

1. Heat Capacity & Latent Heat

Specific Heat Capacity (c)

Energy required to raise the temperature of 1kg of a substance by 1 Kelvin (or 1°C).

Q = mcΔθ

Specific Latent Heat (L)

Energy required to alter the state of 1kg of a substance WITHOUT changing its temperature (e.g., melting ice at 0°C into water at 0°C).

Q = mL

2. Methods of Heat Transfer

  • Conduction: Heat transfer through direct atomic vibrations. Cannot happen in a vacuum. Metals are great conductors due to free "sea of electrons".
  • Convection: Heat transfer via bulk movement of fluids (liquids and gases) caused by density differences when heated.
  • Radiation: Heat transfer via electromagnetic waves (infrared). CAN happen in a vacuum! Dull/black surfaces are the best absorbers and emitters.

3. The Ideal Gas Laws

Boyle's Law

Volume is inversely proportional to Pressure (const Temp).

P&sub1;V&sub1; = P&sub2;V&sub2;

Charles' Law

Volume is proportional to Absolute Temperature (const Press).

V&sub1;/T&sub1; = V&sub2;/T&sub2;

Pressure Law

Pressure is proportional to Absolute Temp (const Vol).

P&sub1;/T&sub1; = P&sub2;/T&sub2;

Remember: In gas laws, temperature MUST always be in Kelvin (K = °C + 273).
