Dynamics Intensive Course

Understanding forces and the causes of motion.

1. Newton's Laws of Motion

  • First Law (Inertia): An object will remain at rest or in uniform motion in a straight line unless acted upon by an external unbalanced force.
  • Second Law (F=ma): The rate of change of momentum is proportional to the impressed force and takes place in the direction of the force. F = m × a
  • Third Law: For every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction force.

2. Friction

Friction opposes relative motion between two parallel surfaces in contact.

F&subr; = μR

Where μ is the coefficient of friction, and R is the Normal Reaction (usually mg on a flat surface).

  • Static Friction: Force keeping a stationary object from moving.
  • Kinetic Friction: Force resisting motion when object is sliding (always less than max static friction).

3. Linear Momentum & Impulse

Momentum (p) is the product of mass and velocity. p = mv (Unit: kg m/s)

Impulse (I) is the change in momentum, or Force multiplied by Time. I = F × t = m(v - u)

Conservation of Linear Momentum

In a closed system with no external forces, the total momentum before collision equals total momentum after collision.
