Waves & Optics Course

Energy transfer through oscillations without bulk transport of matter.

1. Wave Properties

Transverse Waves

Oscillations are perpendicular to wave direction (e.g., Light, Water ripples).

Longitudinal Waves

Oscillations are parallel to wave direction (e.g., Sound waves, Seismic P-waves).

v = f × λ

Velocity = Frequency × Wavelength

2. Wave Behaviors

  • Reflection: Wave bouncing off a boundary. Angle of incidence = Angle of reflection.
  • Refraction: Bending of waves passing from one medium to another due to change in speed.
  • Diffraction: Bending of waves around obstacles or through narrow gaps.
  • Interference: Superposition of two coherent waves causing constructive or destructive effects.

3. The Electromagnetic Spectrum

A continuum of all electromagnetic waves arranged according to frequency and wavelength. All travel at the speed of light c = 3 × 10&sup8; m/s in a vacuum.

Radio → Microwave → Infrared → VISIBLE → UV → X-Ray → Gamma
