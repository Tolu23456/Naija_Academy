Kinematics Course

The geometry of motion without considering its causes.

1. Equations of Uniformly Accelerated Motion (UAM)

When acceleration a is constant, the following four fundamental equations govern an object's motion:

v = u + at (No displacement s)
s = ut + ½at² (No final velocity v)
v² = u² + 2as (No time t)
s = ½(u + v)t (No acceleration a)

Where: u = initial velocity, v = final velocity, a = acceleration, t = time, s = displacement

2. Motion Under Gravity

When an object is in free fall, its acceleration is exactly the acceleration due to gravity, denoted as g.

Convention: By standard WAEC convention, taking upwards as positive:

  • An object thrown vertically upwards has a = -g
  • An object dropped from a height has a = -g (but travels in the negative direction, so signs cancel)
  • At maximum height, v = 0

3. Projectile Motion

Motion in two dimensions under the influence of gravity alone.

Key Formulas:

Time of flight (T): 2u sinθ / g

Maximum Height (H): u² sin²θ / 2g

Horizontal Range (R): u² sin2θ / g

Note: Maximum range is achieved when angle θ = 45°.
