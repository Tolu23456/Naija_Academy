Cell Biology & Processes

Understanding the fundamental unit of life and its functions.

1. Cell Division: Mitosis vs Meiosis

Mitosis

Somatic (body) cell division. Results in exactly 2 identical diploid (2n) daughter cells.

Prophase → Metaphase → Anaphase → Telophase

Meiosis

Gamete (sex) cell division. Results in 4 unique haploid (n) daughter cells through two cycles.

Recombination/Crossing over occurs here!

2. Cellular Transport

How cells move materials across the selectively permeable plasma membrane.

  • Passive Transport: Requires NO ATP (energy). Drives molecules down their concentration gradient (High → Low). Ex: Diffusion, Osmosis, Facilitated diffusion.
  • Active Transport: Requires ATP! Moves molecules AGAINST their gradient (Low → High) using protein pumps (e.g., Na+/K+ pump).
  • Endo/Exocytosis: Bulk transport of macro-molecules via vesicles merging with the membrane.

3. Cellular Respiration (ATP Production)

The aerobic breakdown of glucose to generate energy.

C&sub6;H&sub1;&sub2;O&sub6; + 6O&sub2; → 6CO&sub2; + 6H&sub2;O + ATP (Energy)

Key phases: Glycolysis (Cytoplasm) → Krebs Cycle (Mitochondrial Matrix) → Electron Transport Chain (Inner Membrane). Maximum yield is ~38 ATP.
