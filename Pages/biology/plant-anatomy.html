Plant Anatomy & Physiology

Understanding the green engines of the biosphere.

1. Vascular Tissues

Xylem

Transports Water and dissolved Minerals from the Roots UPWARDS to the leaves. Tissues are essentially dead at maturity (hollow tubes supported by lignin).

Phloem

Transports Glucose and organic nutrients from the leaves to all other parts of the plant (Translocation). Tissues are alive.

2. The Leaf Structure

  • Cuticle: Waxy uppermost layer restricting water loss.
  • Palisade Mesophyll: Densely packed column cells containing the highest concentration of chloroplasts (primary site of photosynthesis).
  • Spongy Mesophyll: Loosely packed cells allowing air spaces for rapid gas exchange.
  • Stomata: Pores usually on the underside governed by Guard Cells. Open for gas exchange but close to stop water loss (Transpiration).

3. Photosynthesis

The conversion of light energy into chemical energy stored in glucose.

6CO&sub2; + 6H&sub2;O + Light Energy → C&sub6;H&sub1;&sub2;O&sub6; + 6O&sub2;

Requires Chlorophyll pigment to trap the photons.
