Human Anatomy & Physiology

The biological systems sustaining human life.

1. The Circulatory System

Double circulation: The heart pumps deoxygenated blood to the lungs (Pulmonary) and oxygenated blood to the body (Systemic).

  • Arteries: Carry blood AWAY from the heart. Thick, muscular walls to withstand high pressure.
  • Veins: Carry blood TOWARDS the heart. Contain valves to prevent backflow.
  • Capillaries: One-cell thick microscopic vessels where gas exchange actually occurs.

2. The Digestive System

Mechanical and chemical breakdown of food for absorption.

Enzyme Location Function
Amylase Mouth (Saliva) Breaks down Starch
Pepsin Stomach (Acidic) Breaks down Proteins
Lipase Pancreas/Sm. Intestine Breaks down Fats/Lipids

3. The Nervous System

Divided into the CNS (Central Nervous System: Brain & Spinal Cord) and PNS (Peripheral Nervous System: Somatic and Autonomic nerves).

The functional unit is the Neuron, which transmits electrical action potentials (impulses) through gaps called Synapses via neurotransmitters.
