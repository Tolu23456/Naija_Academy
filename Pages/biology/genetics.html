Genetics & Heredity Course

The mechanics of inherited traits and chromosomal variations.

1. Mendelian Laws

Gregor Mendel defined inheritance using pea plants. Remember these terminology fundamentals: Allele, Genotype, Phenotype, Homozygous, Heterozygous.

  • Law of Segregation: The two alleles for a trait separate uniformly during gamete formation.
  • Law of Independent Assortment: Alleles of different genes segregate independently of one another.
  • Law of Dominance: When two contrasting alleles are crossed, only the dominant one is expressed in the phenotype.

2. Sex-Linked Traits

Traits controlled by genes located on the sex chromosomes (usually the X chromosome, as the Y carries very few genes).

Common Examples:

Color Blindness and Hemophilia. Because males inherit only one X chromosome (XY), they express sex-linked recessive conditions much more frequently than females (who require two recessive alleles on XX).

3. Genetic Mutations

Sudden, spontaneous changes in the DNA sequence or chromosome structure.

A classic example is Sickle Cell Anemia, caused by a single point mutation in the hemoglobin gene. Interestingly, individuals who are heterozygous (HbA HbS) are somewhat resistant to Malaria!
