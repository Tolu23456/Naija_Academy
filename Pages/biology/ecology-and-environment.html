Ecology Intensive

The interrelationships among organisms and their environments.

1. The Nitrogen Cycle

Nitrogen is essential for amino acids and DNA, but atmospheric N&sub2; is inert. It must be "fixed" into a usable form (Nitrates).

  • Fixation: Lightning or Azotobacter/Rhizobium convert N&sub2; to ammonia/nitrates.
  • Nitrification: Nitrosomonas/Nitrobacter convert ammonia completely to nitrites and nitrates.
  • Denitrification: Pseudomonas convert nitrates back into atmospheric N&sub2; to complete the cycle.

2. Ecological Succession

The gradual and predictable process of species replacement within an ecosystem over time.

Pioneer Species (Lichens) → Intermediate Species (Grasses/Shrubs) → Climax Community (Hardwood Trees)

Primary succession starts on bare rock. Secondary succession occurs where soil already exists (e.g., after a forest fire).

3. Major Biomes

Large geographic regions distinct according to climate, flora, and fauna.

  • Tropical Rainforest: High biodiversity, heavy rainfall, distinct canopy layers.
  • Savanna: Tropical grassland with scattered trees, distinct wet and dry seasons.
  • Desert: Extreme temperature fluctuations, low precipitation, xerophytes.
